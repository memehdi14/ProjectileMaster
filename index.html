Physics Guide

Learn the physics concepts behind Projectile Master

Projectile Motion

Projectile motion is the motion of an object thrown or projected into the air, subject to only the acceleration of gravity.

x(t) = v₀·cos(θ)·t

y(t) = v₀·sin(θ)·t - ½gt²

Where v₀ is initial velocity, θ is launch angle, t is time, and g is gravitational acceleration (9.8 m/s²).

Optimal Launch Angle

For maximum range when landing at the same elevation, the optimal launch angle is 45°.

R = v₀²·sin(2θ)/g

Maximum range occurs when sin(2θ) = 1 (θ = 45°). For different elevations, the optimal angle varies.

Bomb Drop from Moving Plane

When a bomb is dropped from a moving plane, it retains the plane's horizontal velocity while accelerating downward due to gravity.

t = √(2h/g)

d = v·t

Where h is altitude, v is plane speed, t is time to fall, and d is distance to drop before target.

River Crossing with Current

To cross a river with current and land directly opposite the starting point, the boat must point upstream at an angle.

θ = sin⁻¹(v_current / v_boat)

Where θ is the angle to point upstream, v_current is river current speed, and v_boat is boat speed relative to water.

Air Resistance

Air resistance (drag force) opposes motion and depends on velocity, cross-sectional area, and drag coefficient.

F_drag = ½ρv²C_dA

Where ρ is air density, v is velocity, C_d is drag coefficient, and A is cross-sectional area.
